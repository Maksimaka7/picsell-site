What Is Vision AI for Merchandising

Vision AI is a computer vision system that analyses photos of retail shelves taken by field agents on standard smartphones. Instead of a sales rep manually filling in a checklist, the AI automatically recognises products, counts facings, and evaluates planogram compliance — in seconds.

For trade marketing teams, this means four capabilities that were previously impossible to scale:

Share of Shelf Measurement

Vision AI calculates the exact percentage of shelf space occupied by your brand vs. competitors — at every outlet, every visit. No subjective estimation, no forgotten rows. Just objective numbers updated after each agent visit.

Out-of-Stock (OOS) Detection

The system identifies empty shelf slots in real time and can automatically trigger a replenishment order to your ERP — without waiting for the weekly report. Average time from OOS detection to resolution drops from 48–72 hours to under 2 minutes with automated workflows.

Perfect Store Score

Vision AI checks every parameter of your Perfect Store standard simultaneously: product presence, facing count, price tag visibility, POSM placement, planogram compliance. Each outlet receives an objective score — not the one the agent estimated.

Price Compliance Monitoring

The system reads price tags and verifies that recommended retail prices are being respected across the trade network. Discrepancies are flagged automatically, before they erode margin.

Why Manual Audits No Longer Work

Traditional shelf audits have four structural problems that Vision AI eliminates:

✗
Subjectivity. Two agents visiting the same outlet produce different scores. There is no single source of truth — only individual interpretations of the standard.
✗
Speed. A manual audit of one outlet takes 15–20 minutes. Vision AI processes the same information from a photo in under 10 seconds.
✗
Coverage. Agents physically cannot audit every outlet every visit. Spot-checks miss chronic problems that only surface in aggregate data.
✗
Data quality. Manual entry has error rates of 15–30%. Agents under time pressure skip fields, estimate rather than count, or record what should be there rather than what is.
⚠ The hidden cost of manual audits

Research consistently shows that 9 in 10 manual Perfect Store audits contain material errors. Brands are making trade marketing investment decisions — planogram changes, POSM budgets, agent route priorities — based on data they cannot trust.
